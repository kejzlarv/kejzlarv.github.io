### Uncertainty Quantification:
Existence of several competing models to describe the same or similar physical process is a common scenario in many scientific fields. In typical applications, researchers decide on which of these models is the best and proceed with their analysis. This approach, however, ignores one important source of uncertainty associate with existence of multiple models, the so-called model uncertainty. I have been recently working on Bayesian Model Averaging (BMA), which is the natural Bayesian framework when facing a number of alternative models. I am particularly interested in challenges that arise in the context of nuclear physics framework. See the preprint:
- Vojtech Kejzlar, Leo Neufcourt,  Taps Maiti, Frederi Viens. _Bayesian averaging of computer models with domain discrepancies: a nuclear physics perspective_. [arXiv:1904.04793](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.04793}{arXiv:1904.04793)
