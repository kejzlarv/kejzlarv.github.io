### Variational Inference:

This is currently my main research area. I am interested in extending the state-of-the-art _Variational Bayesian_ methods (an alternative to the standard Bayesian approximation of posterior distributions) in the context of calibration of computer models with Gaussian process emulators. Bayesian calibration with Gaussian process emulators, which is a heavily utilized approach for calibration of computer models, becomes infeasible in big-data scenarios with complex and many-parameter models, because it relies on Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithms to approximate posterior densities. On the other hand, variational methods tends to be fast and easy to scale to massive datasets.


### Uncertainty Quantification:
Existence of several competing models to describe the same or similar physical process is a common scenario in many scientific fields. In typical applications, researchers decide on which of these models is the best and proceed with their analysis. This approach, however, ignores one important source of uncertainty associated with existence of multiple models, the so-called model uncertainty. I have been recently working on Bayesian Model Averaging (BMA), which is the natural Bayesian framework when facing a number of alternative models.

### Nuclear Physics:

The field of nuclear physics has been experiencing a robust rump-up of the use of Bayesian statistics in the recent years, especially for the purpose of uncertainty quantification. I am currently involved in several projects with researchers at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams at MSU and Argonne Notational Laboratory. In particular, I work on a robust and systematic methodology for calibration in scenarios with model failures.
